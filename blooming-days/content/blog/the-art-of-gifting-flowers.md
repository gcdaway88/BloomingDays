Flowers have been humanity's universal love language for centuries. But gifting flowers well is an art — it's about reading the moment, knowing the person, and letting the blooms speak for you.

## Start with the occasion

A birthday calls for something cheerful and alive — sunflowers, gerbera daisies, or a wildflower mix. An anniversary deserves something intimate and considered — garden roses, peonies, or a soft monochromatic arrangement. A sympathy arrangement should be calm and understated, not overwhelming.

## Think about the person, not just the flower

Some people love bold colors. Others are drawn to soft, muted tones. If you're not sure, a white-and-green arrangement is universally beautiful and never feels wrong.

## Don't forget the details

A handwritten note matters more than most people realize. The way an arrangement is wrapped — kraft paper with a ribbon, or a simple bundle of stems — sets the tone before a single petal is seen.
